# Generalizable quality signals

This reference lists the signals that generalize across datasets and are safe to use in production. Each signal was verified through a leakage audit that tested whether the signal encodes the label directly or indirectly.

## How we verified these signals

We ran each dataset with five feature subsets: all features, no signal map features, no supplier risk, no signals and no supplier, and raw data only. We compared accuracy and F1 across subsets. Signals that maintained their predictive power when removed from the signal map and computed directly from raw data are genuine. Signals that lost predictive power when removed may be overfit to dataset-specific calibration.

## Verified generalizable signals

These signals are computed directly from the raw Excel data. They do not depend on the status column, the markers column, or the signal map. They generalize across datasets because they measure intrinsic respondent behavior.

**1. LangAssess readability scores.** Top predictor in 9 of 11 datasets. Rejected respondents have higher readability scores, which suggests their open-end text was generated by a language model. The Decipher platform computes this score automatically. Fields: `lang_LangAssessReadLevel`, `lang_LangAssessNumSyl`, `lang_LangAssessNumWords`, `lang_LangAssessNumSen`. Safe to use in production.

**2. Open-end text length.** Top predictor in 6 of 11 datasets. Rejected respondents write shorter open-end answers. Computed from the oe_* fields in the Excel file. Fields: `oe_total_chars`, `oe_mean_chars`, `oe_max_chars`, `oe_total_words`, `oe_max_words`. Safe to use in production.

**3. Matrix straightlining.** Top predictor in 5 of 11 datasets. Rejected respondents give nearly identical answers across grid questions. Computed from the q*r* matrix cells. Fields: `matrix_unique_ratio`, `matrix_most_common_freq`, `matrix_straightline`, `matrix_near_straightline`. Safe to use in production. Check `matrix_unique_ratio` below 0.3 with at least 5 matrix answers.

**4. Completion time.** Top predictor in 4 of 11 datasets. Rejected respondents complete the survey faster. The z-score adjusts for survey length differences. Fields: `qtime_seconds`, `qtime_log`, `qtime_seconds_zscore`. Safe to use in production. Check z-score below negative 1.0 for fast completion.

**5. Coded answer diversity.** Top predictor in 4 of 11 datasets. Rejected respondents give less diverse coded answers and more "don't know" responses. Fields: `coded_count`, `coded_unique_ratio`, `coded_dk_count`, `coded_dk_ratio`. Safe to use in production.

**6. Cross-respondent duplicates.** Top predictor in 3 of 11 datasets. Rejected respondents share user agents or open-end text with other respondents. Fields: `ua_dup_count`, `ua_is_dup`, `oe_dup_count`, `oe_is_dup`, `ip_dup_count`, `ip_is_dup`. Safe to use in production.

**7. Decipher review metadata.** Top predictor in 3 of 11 datasets. The Decipher platform's own review flag. Fields: `rd_RD_Searchr1`, `rd_RD_Searchr3`. Safe to use in production but may not exist on all survey platforms.

## Signals that need caution

**Supplier reject rate.** Top predictor in 5 of 11 datasets. It helps TFG Q1 (adds 1.5 percent accuracy) and TFG Q2, but hurts ODL (drops F1 from 73 percent to 0 percent). Use only when the supplier has at least 20 respondents in the training data. Do not use as the sole signal for discard. Always combine with at least one raw data signal. Field: `supplier_reject_rate`. Computed from the training split only.

## Signals that are NOT generalizable

These signals were removed or flagged as potential leakage:

**Markers column.** Contains "bad:" for rejected respondents. This is essentially a copy of the label. Excluded from all features. Do not use.

**Status column.** The label itself. Used only to create the training target, never as a feature.

**Signal map signals (sig_*).** Generated by a rule-based pipeline that calibrated thresholds using the status column. The signal definitions were influenced by the labels. The leakage audit showed that removing these features did not hurt accuracy on most datasets and improved it on some. Use with caution. Do not rely on them as the primary signal.

**TIER 1/2/3 counts (t1_count, t2_count, t3_count).** Derived from the signal map. Same caution applies.

## Per-dataset signal priority

When analyzing a new dataset, check the most similar training dataset's top signals first. See `per-dataset-ml-signals.md` for the full per-dataset breakdown.

| Dataset type | Check first | Check second |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| Low reject rate (under 10%) | Supplier risk | LangAssess readability |
| Medium reject rate (10 to 30%) | Open-end text length | Matrix straightlining |
| High reject rate (over 30%) | Completion time | Coded answer diversity |
| Conjoint or grid-heavy | Matrix straightlining | LangAssess readability |
| Contractor or B2B | Supplier risk | Open-end text length |
| Consumer CX | LangAssess readability | Completion time |
