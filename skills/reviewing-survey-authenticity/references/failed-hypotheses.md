# Failed or Weak Hypotheses

These signals must not independently drive exclusion.

## Speed Only

A fast respondent can still be real. Speed becomes meaningful only when coupled with semantic incoherence, route problems, implausible text production, matrix abuse, or cross-respondent patterns.

## Shortness Only

Short answers can be acceptable when they directly answer the prompt or name a relevant factor.

## Typo or Grammar Quality

Typos, non-native phrasing, and informal language are not fraud evidence by themselves.

## Polish or AI-Detector Style

Polished text, formal tone, punctuation style, or low typo rate are weak cues. They require grounding analysis and corroborating evidence.

## Straightlining Only

Uniform matrix answers can be valid on homogeneous items or with coherent extreme opinions.

## IP or Device Only

Shared technical identifiers can reflect households, workplaces, public networks, or panel routing. They become stronger only with semantic or behavioral convergence.

## Topic Keyword Mismatch Only

Keyword mismatch is weak until the question contract is clear. A respondent may answer the real prompt with wording outside the seed topic map.
