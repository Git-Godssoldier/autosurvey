# Provisional Hypotheses

Use these as questions to investigate, not as rules.

## Question-Time Elasticity

Authentic respondents often spend more effort on harder prompts than easier prompts. Very flat effort across simple questions, complex matrices, recall tasks, and open ends may indicate automation or inattentive completion.

## Open-End Specificity Gradient

Authentic chains often become more specific when the prompt asks for why, how, or an example. A respondent who stays generic across every opportunity may be weaker.

## Persona Stability

Respondents should sound like the same person across the survey, allowing for prompt changes. Abrupt shifts in vocabulary, viewpoint, product knowledge, or role may indicate pasted or synthetic segments.

## Brand-Funnel Integrity

Claimed awareness, consideration, use, preference, and satisfaction should form a believable path. Contradictions are strongest when paired with open ends that discuss a different brand or category.

## Synthetic Cluster Families

Near-duplicate language, rare phrases, and response-vector similarity may reveal coordinated or generated records. The cluster matters more than any single row’s surface polish.

## Human Friction

Real humans often leave traces of lived friction: qualifying details, imperfect recall, tradeoffs, ordinary complaints, concrete constraints, or context-specific reasons. Absence of friction is not proof, but repeated absence across a suspicious cluster matters.
