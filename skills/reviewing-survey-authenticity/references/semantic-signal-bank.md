# Semantic Signal Bank

These are signal families for agent reasoning. They are not automatic exclusion rules.

## Full-Chain Open-End Authenticity

Ask whether open ends are grounded in the respondent’s own chain:

- Do they reference the selected brand, product, role, task, place, concern, or experience?
- Do they explain why the respondent answered as they did?
- Do multiple open ends share a believable personal viewpoint?
- Are polished answers produced without concrete respondent-specific content?
- Does a later explanation contradict an earlier selection?

Field-level semantic validity comes before protective credit. An answer can be in the general category and still fail the specific field if it gives a numeric code, placeholder, retailer where a product was asked for, product where an ad memory was asked for, off-category brand, platform name, survey-comment answer, or vague adjective that could fit any survey.

Do not allow one valid open end to rescue all other open ends. Judge equipment other-specifies, store other-specifies, ad recall, switching reasons, loyalty reasons, and demographic/professional text separately, then decide whether the total chain is authentic.

## Fabricated Genericity

Look for answers that could be pasted into many surveys without change:

- Empty praise or complaint with no survey-specific content.
- Abstract business language where a personal consumer answer was expected.
- Repeated rhetorical templates across unrelated questions.
- Overly balanced, encyclopedic, or instruction-following prose that avoids lived detail.

## Cross-Question Semantic Drift

Look for respondents who move between incompatible personas:

- Claims use, purchase, familiarity, or ownership that conflicts with screeners or demographics.
- Brand funnels that jump from no awareness to specific experience without explanation.
- Preference explanations that discuss a different prompt, product, category, or decision.
- Recommendation text that does not match satisfaction or use.

## Timing and Effort Plausibility

Timing is probabilistic. It becomes stronger when paired with content:

- Complex grids answered with the same latency pattern as simple items.
- Long, polished answers produced in implausibly little visible time.
- Abrupt paste-like production or sudden style changes across open ends.
- Very fast completion with coherent, grounded, respondent-specific answers can remain acceptable.

## Matrix and Scale Behavior

Uniform matrix behavior is suspicious only in context:

- Stronger concern when items are opposed, unrelated, reverse-coded, or require brand differentiation.
- Weaker concern when the matrix is substantively homogeneous or the respondent has a coherent extreme view.
- Look for cyclic patterns, default-value chains, route-incoherent blanks, and impossible combinations.

In client-rejection calibration, invalid or absent open ends lower the amount of matrix protection available. A row with no human grounding should be held to a stricter standard on top-box concentration, all-brand selection, repeated small shares, and fully uniform ad or recommendation grids.

## Cross-Respondent Homogeneity

Population-level similarity matters after direct row review:

- Repeated rare phrases.
- Matching open-end templates.
- Shared response vectors.
- Shared timing shapes.
- Burst submissions.
- Duplicate technical identifiers that represent multiple supposedly independent respondents.

Shared IP or device evidence can support a case but should not replace semantic review of each full chain.

## Route Integrity and Nonresponse

Resolve skip logic before treating blanks as suspicious:

- Missing prerequisites.
- Populated downstream fields after a disqualifying answer.
- Open ends that answer a prompt the respondent should not have seen.
- Section omissions that look like export defects rather than respondent behavior.

## AI-Assistance Concern

Do not use brittle AI-text tells as standalone proof. Polished prose, formality, low typo rates, and punctuation style are weak cues unless the full chain also lacks grounding, contains contradiction, or matches synthetic clusters.
