# Relationship Patterns

Use these patterns to reason about relationships between questions.

## Parallel

Questions ask similar concepts from different angles. Answers should generally align but do not need to be identical.

## Inverse

One question is the opposite of another. High agreement with both sides needs explanation.

## Prerequisite

A later claim depends on an earlier fact. Specific use, purchase, or satisfaction usually requires awareness or experience unless the survey wording allows hypotheticals.

## Funnel Progression

Awareness, familiarity, trial, use, preference, satisfaction, and recommendation form a chain. Breaks can be normal, but leaps must be plausible.

## Mutually Exclusive

Two answers cannot both be true in ordinary reading. Check whether route, time period, household role, or wording resolves the conflict.

## Temporal

Claims must fit the stated period. A recent purchase, past use, future intent, and lifetime awareness are not interchangeable.

## Numerical

Counts, ranks, totals, ages, income, household size, years, percentages, and ordered preferences should not contradict.

## Routing

Blank fields, populated fields, and downstream answers must fit the route. Distinguish respondent behavior from export defects.

## Open and Closed

The open end should explain, qualify, or complicate the closed answer. Generic repetition is weaker than grounded explanation.
